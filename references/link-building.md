# Backlink & Guest Post Discovery (Linker)

Goal: Discover quality guest post opportunities and build powerful backlinks that boost domain authority.

## Link Building Strategy Overview

Backlinks remain one of Google's top 3 ranking factors. Focus on quality over quantity:
- 1 link from a DR 60+ relevant site > 100 links from low-quality directories
- Relevance matters: A link from a site in your niche is worth more than a generic one
- Anchor text diversity: Mix branded, generic, and keyword-rich anchors

---

## Guest Post Outreach

### Step 1: Find Guest Post Opportunities

Use web search with these proven search operators (search for each):
- `[your niche] "write for us"`
- `[your niche] "guest post"`
- `[your niche] "contribute an article"`
- `[your niche] "guest author"`
- `[your niche] "submit a post"`
- `[your niche] intitle:"guest post guidelines"`

For each result, evaluate:
- Is the site in your niche or closely related?
- Does it appear active (recent posts)?
- Would their audience benefit from your content?

### Step 2: Qualify Opportunities

Before reaching out, check each site for:
- Domain Rating (use a free checker like Ahrefs free tools, Moz, or ubersuggest)
- Organic traffic presence (does it rank for anything in search?)
- Content quality (are existing articles well-written and substantive?)
- Social engagement (do posts get shares/comments?)

**Minimum bar for pursuing:** Active site, relevant niche, appears to have real traffic.

### Step 3: Identify the Right Topic

For each target site:
- Review their content to find gaps you could fill
- Propose a topic that serves THEIR audience (not just your link goal)
- Make sure you can naturally include a link to your own content

### Step 4: Outreach Email Template

Subject: Guest Post Idea for [Site Name] — [Topic]

Hi [Name],

I've been reading [Site Name] for a while and particularly enjoyed [specific recent article]. I noticed you haven't covered [topic gap] in depth — it's something your readers frequently ask about based on the comments I've seen.

I'd love to contribute a guest post: "[Proposed Title]"

It would cover:
- [Key point 1]
- [Key point 2]  
- [Key point 3]

I write for [mention 1-2 relevant publications if any]. Happy to provide writing samples.

Would this be a good fit for [Site Name]?

[Your name]

## Link Reclamation (Easy Wins)

### Unlinked Brand Mentions
Search for your brand name or site name using web search. Find pages that mention you without linking back. Reach out and politely request a link.

Search: `"[your brand name]" -site:[yoursite.com]`

### Broken Link Building
Find resource pages in your niche. Use a broken link checker to find dead links on those pages. Offer your content as a replacement.

Search: `[niche topic] "resources" OR "useful links" OR "helpful links"`

### Competitor Backlink Gaps
Find sites that link to 2+ of your competitors but not to you. These sites clearly link out in your niche and may link to you if you reach out with relevant content.

## Link Types to Pursue (Priority Order)

1. **Editorial links** — Earned naturally by creating link-worthy content (best)
2. **Guest post links** — From contributed articles on niche sites
3. **Resource page links** — Getting listed on curated resource pages
4. **Expert roundup links** — Contributing quotes to expert roundup articles
5. **Broken link replacements** — Replacing dead links with your content
6. **Podcast interview links** — Being a guest on podcasts in your niche

## Link Types to Avoid

- Paid links (violates Google guidelines)
- Links from irrelevant sites (low value, potential penalty)
- Link farms or PBNs (penalty risk)
- Footer/sidebar links on every page of a site (devalued)
- Over-optimized exact-match anchor text patterns

---

## Content That Naturally Attracts Links

Create these content types to earn backlinks without outreach:
- Original research and surveys (journalists link to data sources)
- Free tools and calculators
- Comprehensive ultimate guides
- Infographics and data visualizations
- Industry statistics roundup pages
- Expert interviews and quotes
